UX Research · Usability Study

Washington 211: when a crisis resource is too hard to use in a crisis

A usability study with individuals with Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI) revealed critical navigation and search-relevance failures on Washington 211's housing resource site — where users in crisis were choosing to abandon the site and call in instead. I led research design and execution end-to-end, adapting every phase to the population's needs; WA211 acknowledged the findings, with one team member confirming the search-discoverability fix as a known issue.

01 The Problem
Problem & Barriers

An overburdened call line, and a website that wasn't picking up the slack

WA211 is a statewide crisis resource line connecting Washington residents to housing, food, and health services. As part of a website redesign, WA211 needed to know whether people with cognitive and physical accessibility needs — using individuals with TBI as a proxy population — could actually find housing help on their own.

Barrier: if they couldn't, they'd fall back on an already overburdened call center — and there was no confirmed usability data on whether the redesign would actually fix that.

02 The Strategy
Strategy & Investigation

A study designed around the population being tested, not a standard playbook

Recruiting, screening, and moderating with individuals with TBI meant every stage of the standard usability-study process needed rethinking — from how questions were worded to how much a session could ask of someone before fatigue set in.

Population & Setting

Individuals with TBI served as a proxy population for broader cognitive and physical accessibility needs — common impacts include memory loss, fatigue, light sensitivity, and reduced processing speed, all of which directly shaped session length, task design, and data collection.

Who

6 completed (of 14 scheduled) — individuals with TBI, 1 withdrew mid-session

Where

Remotely via Zoom — laptops, PCs, and mobile devices

Who Executed the Strategy

I ran point on this study end-to-end — from the first stakeholder conversation to the final moderated session — while three teammates contributed across screener drafting, heuristic evaluation, and reporting. (Full team & stakeholder map above.)

03 The Solution

Each finding below is paired with the fix it drove — the causal link from what we observed to what we recommended.

Finding 1 Major · Severity 3

Navigation confusion

Users could not find where to start their search. The primary search function required a non-standard 3-step path, buried in a visually busy, low-contrast homepage.

2/6couldn't find search in time
4/6struggled with location filters
2/6unaware filters existed at all
Visual slot — before/after homepage comparison (pending your photos)
→ Solution
Move the search bar to a fixed position in the global header, above the fold, always visible — eliminating the non-obvious multi-step path.
Finding 2 Critical · Severity 4

Irrelevant search results

After successfully starting a search, results didn't match what participants were looking for — including out-of-state listings for searches explicitly filtered to Washington locations.

4/6received unrelated results
100%out-of-state results in local searches
3/6expressed intent to abandon site
Visual slot — search results screenshot showing the out-of-state mismatch (pending your photos)
"Now, it says Bellingham, Washington, right below the housing apartments. Why am I getting one in San Diego?" — P4, study participant
→ Solution
Implement keyword-matching and mandatory proximity-first ordering, so results actually reflect what users searched for.
04 The Impact
So What

Half of participants said they'd rather call than keep using the site — the exact outcome the redesign was meant to prevent

Confirmed

WA211 acknowledged the search-discoverability finding directly — a team member confirmed it as a known issue on their end, validating the recommendation to move search into the global header.

Acknowledged

The search relevance/irrelevant-results finding was acknowledged as critical by the client, though follow-through on implementation was not confirmed after the engagement ended.
